Your 6-Month Journey

Every step, in order. The order matters - here's why.

1
Get Live
You're posting from Day 1 - not sitting in theory
Source your iPhone and aged email
You need a separate iPhone for your accounts. You need an email that's been around for a while - new emails get flagged. We show you exactly where to get both.
Set up Fanvue account
This is where you make money. Fanvue requires ID verification (KYC) which takes up to 24 hours - get it started now so it's ready when you need it. Once approved, add your Fanvue handle to your integrations on the portal so we can track your progress.
Get your 30-day content pack
We give you 30 days of ready-to-post content. This is what you'll post while you learn. You don't need to create anything yet - just post what we give you.
Create your Instagram account
Using the iPhone and aged email you sourced. We walk you through exactly how to set it up so it doesn't get flagged or banned.
Create your Twitter account
Same thing - using your iPhone and email. Twitter is less strict but we still show you the right way to do it.
Start warmup on Instagram
New Instagram accounts are restricted. You can't just start posting like crazy - you'll get banned. Warmup takes 18 days. You post from the content pack during this time, gradually increasing activity.
Start warmup on Twitter
Twitter warmup is faster - about 7 days. Same idea: gradual activity increase so the platform trusts your account.
Post daily from your 30-day pack
While warmup runs and while you learn the rest of the course, you're posting every day. Building the habit. Growing followers. Not wasting time.
2
Build Your Foundation
Know exactly who you are before you create
Validate your niche
You picked a niche on your onboarding call. Now we make sure it's actually good. Research competitors, find gaps in the market, see what's working. If your niche is shit, we pivot now - not after you've created 100 images.
Build your persona deep
Your AI model isn't just a face. She has a name, age, backstory, personality, voice, lifestyle, interests. The deeper you go, the more real she feels, the more people connect. We give you templates to build this out properly.
Learn how to use the AI tools (Resources section)
Model creation, image generation, video generation - all the technical "how to use the tools" stuff lives in Resources. Go through these when you're ready to create. Tools change, so we update these separately from the main lessons.
3
Create Your Content
Your first batch - it won't be perfect, and that's fine
Set up your Instagram properly
Profile picture, bio, highlights, grid layout. First impressions matter. We show you exactly what a good profile looks like vs a shit one.
Set up your Twitter properly
Same thing - profile, banner, bio, pinned tweet. Different platform, different rules. We cover both.
Create your first batch of images
Profile pics, feed images, Fanvue content. Your first attempt won't be amazing - that's expected. The point is to start creating and get something out there.
Create your first videos
Reels, stories, Twitter videos. Video gets way more reach than images. Again - first attempt, won't be perfect. You'll improve.
Set up your Fanvue profile properly
Bio, banner, intro video, pricing, subscription tiers, automated welcome messages, wall posts. Everything needs to be dialled in before you send traffic there.
Test, post, see what works
Post your content. Watch what gets engagement. Watch what flops. This is data. You're learning what your audience responds to.
4
Get Good
You've been posting shit content to get the ball rolling - now it's time to actually get good
Warmup finishes - switch to your own content
Your 30-day pack runs out around when warmup ends. Perfect timing. Now you're posting the content YOU created. Your accounts are warmed up and ready to grow.
Learn marketing competence
NOW you learn why content actually works. Hooks that stop the scroll. Retention that keeps people watching. Aesthetics that look professional. Positioning that stands out. Trends that are working right now. This is the difference between content that grows and content that flops.
Go back and improve your content
Look at your early content with fresh eyes. It's probably shit - that's expected. Now you know what good looks like. Apply what you learned. Remake it. Level up.
Iterate - test, analyse, improve, repeat
Check your analytics. What's getting reach? What's flopping? Double down on what works. Cut what doesn't. Keep improving. This never stops.
5
Grow & Scale
Content is good enough - now push it
Free marketing strategies
S4S (share for share), collaborations, engagement groups, hashtag strategy, viral content formats. Ways to grow without spending money. We cover all of them.
Paid marketing strategies
Instagram boosts, paid shoutouts, Twitter promotion methods. Your content is good enough to spend money on now. We show you exactly how to do each one without wasting budget.
Expand to more platforms
Threads, TikTok, other platforms. Same content, more reach. Repurpose what you're already making - don't start from scratch.
Create backup accounts
2nd Instagram, 2nd Twitter. Same model, more accounts, more traffic to your Fanvue. Scale horizontally.
6
Build The Business
Turn this into a machine that runs without you
Learn chatting and selling
Subscribers on Fanvue need to be talked to. DMs, selling PPV content, handling objections, building relationships. This is where the real money is made - not just subscriptions.
Optimise your pricing and revenue
Subscription price, PPV pricing, bundles, promos. Small tweaks can double your revenue. We show you what to test and how.
Outsource chatting
When you're getting 2-3+ subscribers per day, you can't chat everyone yourself. Hire chatters or use an agency. We show you how to find, vet, and manage them.
Scale to more accounts
3rd, 4th, 5th accounts. Each one is another traffic source. VAs can manage the backup accounts - you focus on the main one or step back entirely.
Hire your team and build systems
Chatters for DMs. VAs for posting. Content creators if needed. Document everything into SOPs. When your team can follow a system, the business runs without you.

Why This Specific Order?

  • You post BEFORE you're good → builds the habit, warmup runs, you're growing while learning - not stuck in theory for weeks
  • You learn marketing competence AFTER you've created → hits way harder when you can look at your own content and see exactly what's wrong with it
  • You improve THEN spend money → don't waste budget boosting shit content - fix it first, then pay to push it
  • You monetise THEN hire → don't build a team before there's money coming in to pay them
  • You scale accounts AFTER your first one works → prove the model works before multiplying it

The Things That Matter Most

1. Content quality. The students who win are the ones with the best content. Not the most content - the best. Hooks that stop the scroll. Aesthetics that look professional. Positioning that stands out. And it never stops - trends change, algorithms change, you need to stay sharp and keep adapting.


2. Consistency and aggression. You can't take days off. Success requires hustle. Daily posting, daily engagement, daily progress. The good news? We've made it as easy as possible - the 30-day pack, the templates, the systems. But you still have to show up every single day.


3. Stay active and move fast. Be in the Discord daily. Ask questions. Share progress. Learn from other students. Progress through the course as fast as you can without sacrificing quality. Students who disappear for a week then come back never catch up.

6 months of coaching. 6 months of support. 6 months to build a business that runs without you. Make every day count.
